Tech

Fusion 360

I took a CAD and 3D printing class my senior year, and I designed printable, tileable, D&D terrain with modular token bases to hold terrain and objects. The terrain would be held together through magnetic tape, and tokens snap onto the terrain itself. I also modeled and printed a Mimic Dice Box with print-in-place hinges. I enjoyed CAD and Fusion 360, and I am hopeful to learn Blender in the future.

Art

Digital Art

Throughout high school, I took a few digital art classes where we used Adobe Illustrator to create digital art that could be cut out with the Glowforge. One of the designs that I enjoyed making the most was Man Moth, meant to be a companion to my friend’s Moth Man art. My friend, who was taking a screenprinting class, helped me make Man Moth into a T-Shirt where he is safely contained to this day.
